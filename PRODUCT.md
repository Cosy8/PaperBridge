# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Researchers, grad students, and academics exploring the literature. They arrive with a paper or a topic and want to quickly find related work, judge relevance, and follow citations. Context: focused reading sessions, often daytime, scanning many titles and abstracts.

## Product Purpose
PaperBridge takes a Google Scholar article or query and returns ranked recommendations for related papers, fused from semantic (FAISS) and keyword (Elasticsearch) ranking. Success = the user finds genuinely relevant papers fast and trusts the ranking enough to act on it.

## Brand Personality
Calm, credible, precise. It should feel like a well-made research instrument — quiet confidence, no neon, no hype. Three words: trustworthy, legible, focused.

## Anti-references
- "Hacker terminal" dev-tool aesthetic: black background, neon green, monospace everywhere. Reads as a toy, not an instrument, and hurts readability.
- Over-decorated SaaS dashboards (gradient hero metrics, glassmorphism, busy cards).

## Design Principles
- **Readability is the product.** Titles and abstracts are the content; type and contrast serve them first.
- **One quiet accent.** Color marks actions, selection, and relevance — never decoration.
- **Earned familiarity.** Standard, predictable affordances over clever ones.
- **Teach in empty states.** Show what the tool does before the first search.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG 2.1 AA: body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1, visible focus states, real labels on controls, keyboard operable, `prefers-reduced-motion` honored. The graph is supplementary — the ranked list is the accessible source of truth.
